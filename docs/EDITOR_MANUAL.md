# Editor Manual

This guide covers editorial functions in the Ubuntu Journal platform.

## Overview

Editors manage the peer review process:
- Review new submissions
- Assign reviewers to articles
- Make editorial decisions (accept, reject, revisions)
- Track review progress

## Accessing the Editor Dashboard

1. Log in with an editor account
2. Navigate to `/dashboard`
3. Click "Editor Dashboard" or go to `/dashboard/editor`

## Viewing Submissions

The Editor Dashboard shows three types of submissions:

### New Submissions (Awaiting Assignment)
- Status: SUBMITTED
- No reviewers assigned yet
- Ready for desk review

### Under Review
- Status: UNDER_REVIEW
- Reviewers have been assigned
- Waiting for review completion

### Awaiting Decision
- Status: UNDER_REVIEW but all reviews completed
- Ready for editorial decision

## Desk Review

When a new submission arrives:

1. Click on the article title to view details
2. Review the abstract, keywords, and manuscript PDF
3. Decide whether to:
   - **Assign Reviewers**: Proceed with peer review
   - **Desk Reject**: Reject without peer review (out of scope, poor quality)

### Desk Rejection

1. Click "Decision" next to the article
2. Select "REJECTED"
3. Enter a reason (sent to author)
4. Click "Submit Decision"

## Assigning Reviewers

1. From the Editor Dashboard, click "Assign Reviewers" on an article
2. You'll see a list of available reviewers
3. Click "Assign" next to the reviewers you want (typically 2-3)
4. The article status changes to UNDER_REVIEW
5. Assigned reviewers receive notifications

### Finding Suitable Reviewers

The reviewer list shows:
- Name
- Affiliation
- Current active reviews count

Consider:
- Reviewer expertise (based on affiliation)
- Current workload (fewer active reviews = more available)
- Potential conflicts of interest

## Monitoring Reviews

### Review Statuses

- **PENDING**: Reviewer notified, hasn't responded
- **ACCEPTED**: Reviewer agreed to review
- **DECLINED**: Reviewer declined the invitation
- **COMPLETED**: Review submitted

### Handling Declined Reviews

If a reviewer declines:
1. Assign a replacement reviewer
2. Consider reaching out to understand availability

## Making Editorial Decisions

When all reviews are complete:

1. Go to the Editor Dashboard
2. Click "Decision" next to the article
3. Review submitted reviews (recommendations, comments, ratings)
4. Choose a decision:

### Decision Types

**ACCEPTED**
- Article is accepted for publication
- No further revisions required
- Admin can now publish it

**MINOR_REVISION**
- Small changes needed
- Author submits revision
- Usually doesn't require re-review

**MAJOR_REVISION**
- Significant changes needed
- Author submits revision
- May require re-review

**REJECTED**
- Article not suitable for publication
- Provide constructive feedback

### Writing Decision Comments

Include:
- Summary of reviewer consensus
- Specific changes required (for revisions)
- Constructive feedback
- Timeline expectations (for revisions)

**Note**: Comments are sent to authors (excluding confidential reviewer notes).

## Handling Revisions

When an author submits a revision:

1. The article appears in your dashboard with updated status
2. Review the new manuscript
3. Compare with previous version if needed
4. Option to:
   - Send for re-review (assign same or new reviewers)
   - Make final decision

## Communication

### Notifications Sent Automatically

- To reviewers when assigned
- To authors when decision is made
- To authors when article is published

### Manual Communication

For complex situations, contact users directly via email (visible in user profiles).

## Best Practices

1. **Respond promptly** to new submissions (within 1 week)
2. **Assign 2-3 reviewers** to ensure adequate feedback
3. **Monitor review timelines** and send reminders if needed
4. **Provide constructive feedback** even in rejections
5. **Be consistent** in applying quality standards

## Common Scenarios

### All Reviewers Decline

Assign new reviewers. Consider:
- Different reviewers from your network
- Reaching out to authors for reviewer suggestions
- Extending the reviewer pool

### Conflicting Reviews

When reviewers disagree:
- Consider soliciting a third review
- Use your editorial judgment
- Explain the decision clearly to the author

### Author Appeals

If an author disputes a decision:
- Review the reviewers' comments objectively
- Consider having another editor review
- Respond professionally with clear reasoning
