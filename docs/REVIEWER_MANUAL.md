# Reviewer Manual

This guide covers the peer review process for reviewers at Ubuntu Journal.

## Overview

Reviewers play a crucial role in maintaining publication quality:
- Evaluate manuscripts for scientific merit
- Provide constructive feedback to authors
- Recommend acceptance, revision, or rejection
- Maintain confidentiality

## Getting Started

### Becoming a Reviewer

1. Register for an account
2. Contact the editorial office expressing interest
3. Provide your expertise areas and qualifications
4. An admin will update your role to REVIEWER

### Logging In

1. Go to `/auth/login`
2. Enter your credentials
3. Navigate to Dashboard

## Accessing Review Assignments

### Reviewer Dashboard

Go to **Reviewer Dashboard** (`/dashboard/reviewer`)

The dashboard has three tabs:

**Pending Invitations**
- New review requests
- Action: Accept or Decline

**Active Reviews**
- Reviews you've accepted
- Action: Submit your review

**Completed Reviews**
- Past reviews you've submitted
- For reference only

## Responding to Review Invitations

### Accepting a Review

When you receive a review invitation:

1. Go to Reviewer Dashboard → Pending tab
2. Review the article title and abstract
3. Consider:
   - Your expertise in the subject
   - Your availability (typically 2-4 weeks)
   - Any conflicts of interest
4. Click "Accept" to take on the review
5. Download and review the manuscript

### Declining a Review

If you cannot review:

1. Click "Decline" next to the invitation
2. Provide a reason (optional but helpful):
   - Outside expertise area
   - Time constraints
   - Conflict of interest
3. The editor will assign another reviewer

**Note**: Declining is professional and expected when appropriate. It's better to decline than submit a late or poor-quality review.

## Conducting a Review

### Accessing the Manuscript

1. Go to Reviewer Dashboard → Active tab
2. Click "Review" next to the article
3. Download the manuscript PDF
4. Review thoroughly before completing the form

### Review Criteria

Consider these aspects:

**Originality**
- Is the work novel?
- Does it contribute new knowledge?

**Methodology**
- Are methods appropriate and rigorous?
- Is the study design adequate?

**Results**
- Are findings clearly presented?
- Do results support conclusions?

**Writing Quality**
- Is the manuscript well-organized?
- Is language clear and professional?

**Ethical Compliance**
- Are ethical standards met?
- Are limitations acknowledged?

## Submitting Your Review

### The Review Form

1. Click "Review" on your assigned article
2. Complete all sections:

**Quality Ratings** (1-5 scale)
- Originality
- Methodology
- Significance
- Presentation
- Overall Quality

**Recommendation**
- ACCEPT: Suitable for publication as-is
- MINOR_REVISION: Small changes needed
- MAJOR_REVISION: Significant changes needed
- REJECT: Not suitable for publication

**Comments for Authors**
- Detailed, constructive feedback
- Specific suggestions for improvement
- Page/line references when possible
- Professional and respectful tone

**Confidential Comments for Editor**
- Notes not shared with authors
- Concerns about ethics, quality, etc.
- Your confidence in the recommendation

3. Click "Submit Review"

### Writing Effective Comments

**Do:**
- Be specific and constructive
- Reference exact locations (page 3, paragraph 2)
- Suggest improvements, not just problems
- Acknowledge strengths of the work
- Use professional, respectful language

**Don't:**
- Be vague ("needs improvement")
- Be harsh or personal
- Request citations to your own work unnecessarily
- Reveal your identity
- Discuss the review with others

### Example Comment Structure

```
MAJOR COMMENTS:

1. Methods (p. 5): The sample size calculation is not provided. 
   Please include statistical justification for n=200.

2. Results (p. 8, Table 2): The confidence intervals are missing.
   Adding these would strengthen the statistical presentation.

MINOR COMMENTS:

1. Introduction (p. 2, line 15): Citation [12] appears outdated.
   Consider citing more recent systematic reviews.

2. Discussion (p. 11): The limitations section is brief.
   Suggest expanding on generalizability constraints.

POSITIVE ASPECTS:

The research question is timely and relevant. The methodology
is generally sound and the writing is clear.
```

## Timeline Expectations

- **Response to invitation**: 3-5 days
- **Complete review**: 2-4 weeks from acceptance
- **Revision re-review**: 1-2 weeks

If you need more time, contact the editor.

## Confidentiality

As a reviewer, you must:

- Keep manuscripts confidential
- Not share content with others
- Not use ideas from manuscripts before publication
- Not contact authors directly
- Disclose any conflicts of interest

## Conflicts of Interest

Decline a review if you:

- Have collaborated with authors recently
- Have a financial interest in the research
- Work at the same institution as authors
- Have a personal relationship with authors
- Feel you cannot be objective

## After Submission

### Review Outcomes

You may not always learn the final decision. The editor weighs all reviews and makes the final call.

### If Asked to Re-Review

For revised manuscripts:
1. You'll receive a notification
2. Review the revision and author's response
3. Submit an updated recommendation

## Best Practices Summary

1. **Respond promptly** to invitations
2. **Be thorough** in your evaluation
3. **Be constructive** in your comments
4. **Be timely** in completing reviews
5. **Be confidential** with manuscript content
6. **Be objective** in your assessment
